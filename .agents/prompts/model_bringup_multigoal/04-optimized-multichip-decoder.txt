/goal Use $optimize for the HF_MODEL multichip decoder in the current tt-metal checkout.

This goal creates the optimized-multichip-decoder state for the repo-local TTNN autoport pipeline. Start from the completed multichip decoder and optimize that multichip path in place; do not begin full-model or vLLM work in this goal.

Goal completion requirements:
- The measured path is the multichip decoder path on the target mesh, not a single-chip or replicated fallback unless such a fallback is explicitly proved to be the best feasible option.
- Multi-chip prefill and decode PCC remain at the accepted baseline for every meaningful layer kind.
- Warmed prefill and traced warmed decode latency are reported before and after this multichip optimization pass.
- tt-perf-report advice-backed human-readable tables and CSV/provenance logs exist for the multichip path, and the report records which advice was followed or rejected with evidence.
- Async CCLs, fused matmul-CCL opportunities, collective placement, semaphore/preallocated-buffer reuse, residual layout, activation sharding, DRAM-sharded decode matmuls, and precision/fidelity reductions are tried where supported or explicitly ruled out with exact TTNN/API/hardware evidence.
- The final path avoids unnecessary gather/reshard/all-reduce between decoder layers unless measured evidence shows it is faster overall.
- Runtime fallback audit is clean, stress or repeated-run coverage matches risk, and watcher-clean evidence exists for the optimized multichip path.
- models/autoports/<model>/doc/optimized_multichip_decoder/README.md and work_log.md summarize commands, before/after PCC and perf, CCL findings, tt-perf-report conclusions, rejected options, limitations, and exact artifacts.
- The checklist in the $optimize skill has been completed with evidence
- The multichip decoder has been optimized as far as practical on the current hardware. If an optimization is left undone, keep working; do not defer it.
- For MoE models, the final path keeps gate-selected active-expert execution; dense all-expert execution is not a final implementation. It is only acceptable as a temporary diagnostic or as a documented blocked
  fallback after active-expert paths have been debugged with exact evidence.

Do not mark this goal blocked just because PCC, trace replay, watcher, profiler, CCL, cache, MoE or other validation currently fails. Those failures are expected bringup work. Before marking blocked, isolate the failure to the smallest reproducible component, compare against the optimized single-chip baseline, try a concrete fix/refutation loop, and for subtle PCC, mesh/CCL, cache, or routed-expert bugs use $autofix or document why those skills could not be used. A blocked result requires evidence that continued debugging by the agent is no longer meaningful, not merely that a requirement is still failing.
