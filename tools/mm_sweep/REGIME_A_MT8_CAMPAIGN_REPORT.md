## 5. Recommended next optimization (causal)

**Efficiency vs Mt** (median %512): Mt1=91%, Mt2=89%, Mt4=81%, Mt8=59% — efficiency falls sharply into the Mt=8 regime.

**Primary finding — the picker's cost-model fallback systematically mis-picks on the M-scaling shapes (which use the fallback, not the 20-shape lookup table):**
1. **Prefer M-split (Sm>1) over deep split-K on low-arithmetic-intensity Mt>=4 shapes.** 10 flagged shapes want a higher Sm than the picker chose (e.g. 256x2048x512 Sm1->Sm3 +25%, 256x15360x1536 Sm1->Sm2 +26%). Ablation root cause: at high Pk the split-K **reduction** dominates (no_reduce -26..-38% on the Pk8 shapes) and in0-**forwarding** is heavy (skip_in0_fwd -19..-30%); trading K-split for M-split (now cheap post-in1_near) removes both. This is the single biggest lever.
2. **Prefer kb>=2 and nsb>=3 on deep-K / wide shapes.** 11 flagged Sm=1 shapes want deeper K-blocks / more N sub-blocking than the fallback's kb=1,nsb=1 (e.g. 32x15360x768 [1,12,1,1,1]->[1,6,1,2,3] +24%: ablation shows this shape is **in1-read bound (skip_in1_read -73%)** and kb>=2 cuts per-block read overhead; 256x2304x6144 nsb 1->3 +22%).

**These are PICKER (offline cost-model) changes, not kernel changes** — the fixes are already expressible in the existing config space. Recommend: (a) add an M-split branch to the cost-model for low-AI Mt>=4, (b) raise the kb/nsb floor in the fallback, (c) extend the lookup table to the M-scaling shapes with the measured winners. Land only after a stability + full-corpus regression pass (this campaign did not edit the picker).

**CB1-depth experiment (optimization #5): NOT warranted by the evidence.** in1 backpressure is not the exposed bottleneck — the in1-bound shape (32x15360x768) is read-*volume* bound (fixed by kb>=2, a config choice), and the low-AI shapes are reduction/forwarding bound (skip_in1_read only -3..-12% there). A deeper CB1 would not help a shape whose critical path IS the DRAM read. Deferred unless a future shape shows in1 backpressure with compute headroom.

**Padding (optimization #6):** balanced-tail shapes show sched/valid ~1.0-1.06 (small); the Section-1 table's delivered-vs-effective BW gap is minor, so skipping padded compute is low-value for this corpus.
