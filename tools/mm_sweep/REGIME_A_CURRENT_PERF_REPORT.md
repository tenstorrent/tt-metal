## Headline finding — no NEW material gap warranting a fresh experiment

The characterization reproduces the established picture, cleanly and PCC-verified. Efficiency is high for
high-arithmetic-intensity shapes (deep-K / wide-N: 84–98% %512, wall/ideal ~1.0–1.1) and degrades
monotonically with Mt only for the **low-AI small-N shapes** (K=2048, small N): median %512 falls
92→89→83→71% for Mt=1→2→4→8, and the matched-M table shows the worst family (2048×512) drop 60→41%512.

**Every worst-underperformer is the same, already-investigated class:** low-AI K=2048 (and 6144×768) shapes
at Mt≥4, which are **in1-read-bound (BRISC critical) with 47–59% core-spread** (load imbalance across the
split-K/M-split cores). This is exactly the regime the split-K reduction-tree, in0-chunk-streaming, and
tiny-shape-path experiments already targeted and rejected (each concluded these shapes are at the practical
floor: fixed dispatch + in1-read + ring/reduction fill-drain on tiny work — not a fixable dataflow choice).
Their absolute excess is small (3–21 µs on 7–46 µs walls), so per your guidance they are not "bad" in a way
that warrants more delivery/reduction tuning. **No shape shows a material, reproducible gap beyond this known
class**; the remaining lever for these shapes is trace/dispatch amortization or op-fusion (model level), not
a new regime-A kernel. (Mt=16 is far worse — wall/ideal 1.6–2.6 — but it is an unoptimized diagnostic regime
the picker does not target; see the diagnostic section.)
