## Anomaly Ledger

- Observed anomaly: the first review found that the prose claimed HiFi4 sparse
  experts while the measured sparse rows were LoFi.
  Evidence: the final source now constructs
  `ep_sparse_compute_kernel_config` from an explicit `lofi|hifi2|hifi4`
  control and passes it at every packed/split gate/up/down sparse call; the
  runtime fallback audit asserts all four call sites receive it. Current
  `prefill_report.csv` rows 488/504/554 and current decode sparse rows report
  `LoFi BF16 x BFP8/BFP4 => BF16`, matching `final_default_perf_seq128.json`.
  Affected path: selected EP4 active-expert prefill and traced decode.
  Control or comparison: sparse-only HiFi2 and HiFi4 both pass both meaningful
  layer kinds but measure 20.689783/0.504293 ms and
  20.984613/0.509997 ms versus the current LoFi default at
  20.583611/0.503275 ms.
  Likely subsystem: sparse-matmul compute-kernel configuration and reporting.
  Investigation performed: inspected source call sites, current profiler rows,
  performance JSON, PCC JUnit/logs, and `AUTOFIX_SPARSE_FIDELITY.md`.
  Resolution: fixed; explicit LoFi is the fastest accepted real-weight policy
  and is documented consistently.

- Observed anomaly: the original topology audit incorrectly dismissed fused
  all-gather plus local-output O, and its first padded-N=736 implementation
  failed on receiver rank 3.
  Evidence: `AUTOFIX_MISSING_TOPOLOGIES.md`, the retained off-default
  `all_gather_minimal_matmul_async` source path, and the `fused_o_ag_*`
  artifacts. The isolated padded result localized rank 3 at PCC 0.982617;
  adapting to the smallest legal natural N=720 shape passed every rank at
  0.999997 and both real layer kinds. The correct BF16 whole-layer family then
  measured 20.771860/0.642176 ms, slower than the final default. BFP8 passed
  the isolated O boundary but failed full-attention prefill at 0.989274.
  Affected path: TP4 attended-input gather plus rank-local O output projection.
  Control or comparison: selected local-input/full-output O plus internal ring
  all-reduce at 20.583611/0.503275 ms.
  Likely subsystem: fused Ring receiver/output handoff for the padded 736-wide
  rank-3 shard.
  Investigation performed: adapted rank/layout/persistent buffer/weight
  packing, isolated all receivers, retried the smallest legal output shape,
  and ran real-layer PCC and traced whole-layer timing.
  Resolution: controlled and evidence-backed rejection; the alternative
  decomposition is expressible and correct at BF16 but 27.6% slower in decode.

- Observed anomaly: the first residual probe restored the same layer's whole
  expert input and did not prove that an EP output fracture could reach the
  next input norm and packed QKV.
  Evidence: `fractured_ep_to_qkv_bfloat16.json` and
  `fractured_ep_to_qkv_bfloat8_b.json` retain the exact real-weight boundary:
  active EP partial -> padded persistent RS -> local736 residual -> distributed
  RMSNorm with 2944/2880 statistics correction -> persistent fused AG plus real
  packed QKV. BF16 minimum PCC is 0.999993 and measures
  0.539213 ms versus 0.452406 ms replicated; BFP8 minimum PCC is 0.999859 and
  measures 0.526221 ms versus 0.452890 ms replicated.
  Affected path: inter-layer residual contract and the next attention consumer.
  Control or comparison: the final replicated BF16 layer boundary, which needs
  no inter-layer collective or conversion.
  Likely subsystem: residual topology, distributed normalization, CCL payload,
  and fused gathered-input QKV.
  Investigation performed: fixed the missing-layer fixture lookup and a
  diagnostic shard-padding error without changing candidate math, then traced
  100 replays for BF16 and BFP8 with persistent buffers.
  Resolution: fixed as an evidence gap and rejected on measured latency. The
  lower-movement family was measured through the real next consumer without an
  immediate old-contract restore.

- Observed anomaly: the first review found no same-run reconciliation for the
  profiled wall/device gap and no roofline.
  Evidence: current `perf/final/perf_accounting.json` uses the same three-replay
