1 · Why KDA is easy to distribute

Per-head independent

No cross-device comms inside the delta rule — heads partition across TP, the recurrence runs locally. Head-parallelism is nearly free.

Projections dominate (95%)

Per token: 39.5 M MACs of q/k/v/o/gate projections vs 2.1 M of recurrence. The heavy part distributes like a normal TP dense layer.

Fixed-size state

State is [heads, K, V], independent of sequence length — so sequence-parallelism exchanges a constant amount of data, unlike attention's growing KV.

2 · The mesh & the contract KDA must honor

Production sparse-MLA (the family Kimi-Linear belongs to) runs on SP=8 × TP=4, mesh (8,4) = (sp_size, tp_size), axis 0 = SP (sequence), axis 1 = TP (heads) — hard-asserted in the MLA code. TP=4 shards the 32 heads → 8 heads/chip; SP=8 shards the sequence into 8 spans.

5 · The one genuinely-new piece — SP state-scan

MLA's SP axis all-gathers the KV cache (re-materializes the whole sequence — the flagged prefill bottleneck). KDA needs no such thing: the recurrence carries a fixed-size state, so SP over KDA is a scan of that state along axis 0.

  1. Local, fully parallel — each SP rank runs the chunked recurrence over its span → local output + a local state-transition (Ā_span, correction).
  2. Scan across axis 0 (8 ranks, line) — combine to get each span's incoming prefix state. Exchanged object = [8 heads, 128, 128] fp32 = 512 KB/boundary; 7 line hops ÷ 400 GB/s ≈ 9 µs.
  3. Apply prefix correction locally — add the q·S_prefix term to each span's output.

So KDA's sequence-parallelism is structurally cheaper than MLA's — fixed-size state, not the growing cache. Start with a simple sequential line-scan (9 µs is already negligible at SP=8); an associative log-depth scan is a later refinement.

6 · Full-model sketch (consistency)

On the shared (8,4) mesh, attention and MoE use different parallelism on the same devices, reconciled by collectives at the block boundary. KDA introduces no new boundary collective:

KDA layers (3/4): TP=4 shards 32→8 heads, SP=8 state-scan. MLA layers (1/4): unchanged. MoE (every layer): expert-parallel, all_to_all on axis 0. The only new primitive anywhere is the KDA SP-axis state-scan.

7 · Open questions / needs measurement

  1. Compute FLOP/s (50–100 TFLOP/s) — the C:DM ratio depends on it; confirm with a Tracy dump of the TP=4 projection matmuls at 8 heads/chip.
  2. TP=4 matmul efficiency at 8 heads/chip — large enough to be efficient, or dispatch-bound?
  3. State-scan implementation — sequential line vs associative parallel; whether to fuse it into the chunk kernel.
  4. Chunk size × SP interplay — per-span chunked scan must tile into T/SP; interaction with the MLA stack's block-cyclic SP seq layout.
  5. Which tree Kimi-Linear integrates into — the (8,4) vs (4,8) convention inconsistency.
  6. Decode (out of scope) inverts the balance to latency/collective-bound — a separate mapping analysis when decode is prioritized.
