02Q1 — what MeshShape(1,16) means

MeshShape(1, 16) is a logical grid of 1 row × 16 columns — i.e. a straight line of 16 chips, indexed 0–15. The code moves data along it with topology=ttnn.Topology.Linear.

It is a line — not a ring, not a torus. There is no wrap-around link from chip 15 back to chip 0 (that would be Topology.Ring). Chips 0–7 live on node-b, chips 8–15 on node-a; the two halves join through four cross-host QSFP cables.

node-a · rank 1 · chips 8–15

03Q2 — the real 16-chip wiring

Physically it is not one flat line. Each T3K is 8 chips arranged as a 2×4 mesh (4× n300 boards, 2 chips per board). The two T3Ks are bridged by four QSFP cross-host cables — one from each MMIO chip (0–3), 2 channels each = 8 links. The runtime flattens all of this into the logical line above and routes over it with FABRIC_2D.

Each edge is actually two ethernet channels (for bandwidth). Within a T3K the four n300 boards are {0,7} {1,5} {2,6} {3,4} — the two chips on a board are joined by an on-board trace; the four MMIO/PCIe chips 0–3 form a ring (0-1-2-3-0), and each one carries a QSFP cable to the matching chip on the other host. An all_gather on the logical line therefore hops chip→neighbor across the 2×4 grid and crosses a QSFP cable to reach the other host.
