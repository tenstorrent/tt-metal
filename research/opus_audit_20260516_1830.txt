## Root Cause Analysis

### Failure chain (Session 2 -> tt-smi -> Session 3)

1. Session 2 (1x4 mesh, 2CQ): wait_for_fabric_workers_ready timed out
   - FIX AI global deadline forced reset of 20 channels
   - FIX DK-2 processed MMIO channels serially (500ms each, 17:01:11-17:01:14)
   - FIX XZ confirmed all 12 MMIO channels rebooted in "0ms" — GENUINE (>10s elapsed since deassert due to non-MMIO processing)

2. RiscFirmwareInitializer::teardown(): FIX DU correctly skipped all 25 MMIO channels (already at UMD firmware). Zero FIX AC resets. Clean teardown.

3. Runner script at 17:01:35: Detects "T3K topology may have degraded mid-run", runs tt-smi -r. This puts ALL ERISCs back into ROM boot.

4. Session 3 (MeshDevice1x4Fixture) at 17:01:52:
   - All MMIO channels at ROM postcode 0x49705180
   - FIX BT promotes all to probe_dead
   - FIX RR soft-resets all 24 simultaneously
   - FIX BH polls 5000ms -> ALL 24 fail
   - Cause: simultaneous boot contention + ETH link training with ROM-state non-MMIO peers

### Key insight
The tt-smi reset between test binaries (triggered by runner script for any rc=1) undoes ALL careful teardown work. ERISCs are put back into ROM. 24 simultaneous soft-resets compete for ETH link training bandwidth — deadlock.

## Recommendations

1. PRIMARY: The tt-smi reset between test binaries is the proximate cause of Session 3 failure. The runner script's "topology may have degraded" detection is too aggressive — it triggers tt-smi for any rc=1, even when teardown was clean. Consider: skip tt-smi if the last session's teardown completed without relay_path_broken flag.

2. SECONDARY: FIX BH 5000ms timeout may be too short for 24 simultaneous channel boots post-tt-smi. Options:
   - Increase to 8000-10000ms
   - Implement staggered reset (batches of 4-6 channels)
   - Add post-tt-smi awareness: if run_launch_phase detects tt-smi occurred, use a longer timeout budget

3. OBSERVATION: FIX DS and FIX DU both worked correctly in this run. The fixes are validated — the failures are caused by external infrastructure (tt-smi), not by these fixes.
