# learn-ltx-audio-opt-playbook

## LTX-2 audio decode opt playbook (bh Galaxy 4x8)
**2026-06-10 07:47** ¬∑ `tt-metal@d0b24d52937-dirty`

**Core insight:** The decode is compute-bound in the conv3d MAC of the two BigVGAN vocoders (main + BWE), not in CCL. The fp32 conv3d blocking table is the single highest-payoff lever; the 4x8 per-chip shard shapes miss every tuned `_FP32_BLOCKINGS` row anyway, so the win is "add table entries," not "rewrite kernels." BWE generator is ~54%/~680ms (#46423). Levers ranked at bottom.

**Hot-spot walk (Vocoder upsample stack, ch 1536‚Üí768‚Üí384‚Üí192‚Üí96‚Üí48‚Üí24):**
- Per stage i: one `ConvTranspose1dViaConv3d` (ups[i]) + 3√ó `AMPBlock1`. Each AMPBlock1 = 6 `DilatedConv1d` (convs1 k‚àà{3,7,11}√ód‚àà{1,3,5}, convs2 k=3) + 4 `Activation1d` (`vocoder_ltx.py:AMPBlock1.forward`).
- Each `Activation1d` = UpSample1d(2√ó) ‚Üí SnakeBeta ‚Üí DownSample1d(2√ó) (`audio_resample.py:Activation1d.forward`). Up/Down are depthwise FIR (`audio_ops.py:depthwise_tap_filter`, k=12), now one `ttnn.experimental.conv1d_depthwise` tilized matmul each (`audio_ops.py:208 _USE_CONV1D_DEPTHWISE`).
- FLOP dominance: the channel-mixing `Conv1dViaConv3d` (`audio_ops.py:Conv1dViaConv3d.forward`, the conv3d MAC, C√óC per tap) dwarfs the depthwise FIRs and SnakeBeta elementwise. The 18 convs/AMPBlock √ó 3 blocks √ó 6 stages = ~108 channel-mix convs are the realistic targets, with the wide stages (768,768 k11 and 384,384) costliest. ConvTranspose (only 6, gathers T to full + runs unsharded ‚Äî `audio_ops.py:868`) is secondary.
- Verify ranking on device: `prof_vocoder_forward.py::test_prof_vocoder_categories -k bh_4x8` (category breakdown) and `::test_prof_vocoder_per_conv` (per (Cin,Cout,K) ms).

**Fix A ‚Äî keep T in ROW_MAJOR through the partition (kill align=32√ófactor inflation):**
- `_host_to_device` (`vocoder_ltx.py:402-411`) pads T to `align=32*factor`; at factor 8 ‚Üí 256, so real 601 ‚Üí 768 (+28%) feeding every conv. Goal: pad only to `factor` (so `mesh_partition` splits evenly) and let per-chip T be non-tile (601‚Üí608, +1%).
- `_forward_device` (`:422-425`) currently `TILE ‚Üí _partition_t ‚Üí ROW_MAJOR`. `_partition_t` (`audio_ops.py:256`) is just `ttnn.mesh_partition(dim=1)`; **mesh_partition on the T (row) dim does not require TILE** ‚Äî partition directly in ROW_MAJOR and drop the two `to_layout`s. Per-chip T then = `ceil_to(601)/8` which need not be a tile multiple.
- Blast radius ‚Äî who tolerates non-tile per-chip T in ROW_MAJOR: `_t_neighbor_pad` halos (`audio_ops.py:131`, dim-1 neighbor_pad, row-dim, fine), `Conv1dViaConv3d`/`conv1d_depthwise` (consume ROW_MAJOR (B,T,1,1,C), T is the free dim, fine), `_zero_stuff_t`/`_zero_pad_t`/`_replicate_pad_t` (row concats, fine). Who needs TILE: the `_all_gather_t` at the very end (`:484-487`, already wraps TILE‚ÜîROW_MAJOR ‚Äî keep), the per-op `_tpad_mask` (`audio_ops.py:316` builds mask in TILE then partitions ‚Äî switch its `global_T` math + slice index from tile-padded to real per-chip T), and `SnakeBeta` (`audio_ops.py:1072` upcasts to TILE itself, unaffected).
- Minimal correct change: (1) `_host_to_device` align=`factor` not `32*factor`; (2) `_forward_device` partition in ROW_MAJOR (no TILE wrap); (3) audit `_set_tpad_tail`/`_tpad_mask` (`:337,:316`) ‚Äî the `idx=(global_T-tpad_image-1)%local_T` and `local_T*factor` reconstruction assume uniform per-chip T (still holds if `mesh_partition` splits evenly), but `tpad_image` shrinks so masks change; re-derive. Gate: `test_audio_components_ltx::_assert_sharded_matches_unsharded` (max|Œî|<5e-3, `:131`) MUST stay green ‚Äî t_pad boundary drift is exactly what it catches.

**conv3d blockings for Galaxy:** `_FP32_BLOCKINGS` (`conv3d.py:483`) is keyed ONLY by `(Cin,Cout,(k,1,1))` ‚Äî NOT mesh/T/per-chip shape (`get_conv3d_config:557-564`). fp32 path skips the `(h,w,Cin,Cout,k,T,H,W)` `_BLOCKINGS` table entirely. Existing rows are 2x4-derived (channel chain there is 768‚Üí384‚Üí192‚Üí96‚Üí64‚Üí32, with C-TP factor 2). On 4x8 the C-TP factor is 4: gathered Cin is full (768/384/‚Ä¶) but the **column-parallel out shard is Cout/4** (`audio_ops.py:678 out_channels_shard`, `_pick_c_out_block_shard`). Keys still match on full (Cin,Cout) so lookup hits ‚Äî BUT the tuned `C_out_block`/`T_out_block` were swept for the factor-2 out-shard; with a factor-4 shard the conv either keeps a too-large block or the entry simply doesn't exist for the 1536‚Üí768 conv_pre/ups widths, so it falls to `(32,32,1)` default (`:564`, T_out_block=1 ‚Üí one dispatch per T-row = worst case). Action: sweep + add `_FP32_BLOCKINGS` rows for the 4x8 channel pairs at the larger T_out_block the longer per-chip T now affords (e.g. (1536,768,(11,1,1)), (768,384,(4,1,1)) and the AMP (768,768,‚Ä¶)/(384,384,‚Ä¶) keys with bigger T_out_block). Because the key is channel-only, you can't have 2x4 and 4x8 rows coexist for the same (Cin,Cout,k) ‚Äî either re-key the fp32 table to include factor, or run 4x8 with its own table. Re-key is the clean fix.

**BWE channel-TP factor-4 crossover:** BWE generator = Vocoder(upsample_initial_channel=512, rates [6,5,2,2,2]) ‚Üí channel chain 512‚Üí256‚Üí128‚Üí64‚Üí32‚Üí16 (`test_audio_components_ltx.py:95`). `pipeline_ltx.py:1525-1532`: `use_bwe_ctp = env LTX_BWE_CHANNEL_TP if set else channel_parallel.factor>2`; factor-2 (2x4) loses (+135ms gather tax), factor‚â•4 (4x8) is the intended win. Trade: every channel-mixing conv pays an all-gather of Cin (`Conv1dViaConv3d.forward:772 gather_channel_to_full`) but computes Cout/4. Caveat: `channel_align_unit=32*factor=128` at factor 4 (`audio_ops.py:50`), so late stages pad hard ‚Äî 32ch‚Üí128 (4√ó), 16ch‚Üí128 (8√ó!). The aligned() pad inflates the matmul on exactly the cheap small-C tail stages, eroding the win. Measure: `prof_vocoder_forward.py::test_prof_bwe_per_conv` plus a full Stage-C wall with `LTX_BWE_CHANNEL_TP=0` vs `=1` on 4x8; expect early/wide stages (512,256,128) net faster under C-TP, late stages (32,16) net slower from the 128-pad ‚Äî the question is whether the wide-stage win > the gather tax + tail pad. If the tail dominates, gate C-TP off for stages with C<128 (run those single-axis). Bit-identical to single-axis on real weights per the comment.

**Device-profiler zone-marker abort:** `test_prof_vocoder_devicetime` (`prof_vocoder_forward.py:210`) wraps each AMPBlock1 to call `ttnn.ReadDeviceProfiler(mesh)` mid-forward (`:247`). A flush mid-graph reads the device zone buffer while CCL/halo (`neighbor_pad_persistent_buffer`) or the trailing `_all_gather_t` zone is still open across the mesh ‚Üí "End marker found without a corresponding start marker" on dump (a start zone got truncated by the prior flush window). Fix for a clean per-op DEVICE FW CSV: drop the per-AMPBlock flush entirely and rely on the single end-of-forward `ReadDeviceProfiler` (`:262`) ‚Äî one short forward at small `AUDIO_T_FRAMES` (e.g. 64‚Äì120) stays under the ~1000-zone tracy buffer (the buffer-overflow was the original reason for the periodic flush; shrinking T removes the need). If still over, exclude the CCL ops by profiling unsharded (factor 1) or just the AMP convs. Invoke: `cd /home/smarton/tt-metal && TT_METAL_HOME=$(pwd) python -m tracy -p -r -m pytest models/tt_dit/tests/models/ltx/prof_vocoder_forward.py::test_prof_vocoder_devicetime -k bh_4x8`. **Ranked levers (payoff √ó risk):**
1. **Add 4x8 fp32 conv3d blockings (+ re-key table by factor)** ‚Äî highest payoff (touches all ~108 main + ~90 BWE convs, kills the (32,32,1) fallback), low-med risk (pure config; numerically identical). Gate: re-run components test, not strictly the shard gate (blocking doesn't change math). DO FIRST after a sweep.
2. **Fix A (ROW_MAJOR T partition, align=factor)** ‚Äî high payoff (~28%‚Üí~1% T inflation = less work in every conv at factor 8), MED risk (touches tpad mask math; the exact thing the shard gate guards). MUST keep `_assert_sharded_matches_unsharded` green.
3. **BWE channel-TP gating tune (per-stage C-TP off below C<128)** ‚Äî med payoff (claws back the 16/32ch tail pad tax), low risk (gating only, bit-identical). Measure with `LTX_BWE_CHANNEL_TP=0/1` first.
4. **Profiler flush fix** ‚Äî enabling lever, near-zero payoff itself but unblocks per-op attribution for 1‚Äì3. Trivial risk.
